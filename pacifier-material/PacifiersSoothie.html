Do soothie pacifiers cause gas?

Pacifiers are one of the less significant reasons of gas production, albeit they are not the main culprits. Natasha Burgert, M.D., stated in Forbes that "babies can swallow air during feeding, when using a pacifier, and while sobbing." However, gas can develop if the infant repeatedly sucking on the pacifier and consumes air. Dr. Burgert said, "The swallowed air gets trapped in the gut and creates discomfort. Examine the pacifier to lessen the amount of air inhaled.

Experts offer their best postures and techniques for easing newborn gas:

- Burp your infant both before and after a feeding to make sure that any air they may have swallowed is released. Gas can be produced during breastfeeding and bottle feeding if the baby does not have the proper latch.

- After feeding, keep them standing. Keep your infant upright even after you've burped them so that their breathing can continue normally despite having a full belly. Additionally, keeping them upright lessens the possibility of reflux.

- Practice belly time. Tummy time is a great approach to help our babies with any gas they may experience throughout the day. No matter how long, having newborns move around by using their body power helps them expel out any gas.

Do soothie pacifiers cause problems with teeth?

Here are a few potential problems that pacifiers may cause:

- Open bite: Even when the mouth is closed, the teeth may not completely close or touch in this type of malocclusion, or misalignment. Additionally typical are larger gaps between the front teeth.

- A pacifier may also contribute to overbite. With the teeth pointing outward, it is a malocclusion that is quite similar to an open bite. However, in this instance, the upper teeth far more than naturally cover the bottom teeth.

- Buck teeth: In some circumstances, projecting front teeth, or buck teeth, are genetic and cannot be avoided. However, the disease can also be brought on by thumb sucking and using a pacifier.

Modifications to the mouth's roof: Prolonged usage of pacifiers might lead to a narrowing or overall malformation of the mouth's roof.

How to sterilize soothie pacifiers?

Just before use When you remove the pacifiers from their packaging, don't forget to sanitize them! The pacifiers are placed in plenty of hot water for 5 minutes to do this. Both silicone and latex pacifiers fall under this category. In rare instances, natural rubber latex may spill over the shield, thus it's crucial to sanitize in lots of water. We also advise sterilizing latex pacifiers separately. Only prior to first usage does this apply.

Using reprimands to sterilize

Following, we advise using the following technique to clean both silicone and latex pacifiers: Place the teethers in a fresh bowl. Over the pacifiers, pour hot water. Allow the pacifiers to soak for around five minutes. Pick them up and place them on a fresh towel to dry.

Microwave sterilization for sanitization

Be mindful that latex will disintegrate if the temperature rises above 100°C/212°F. Therefore, we do not advocate sterilizing natural rubber latex in a microwave. Unlike latex, silicone can withstand higher temperatures. Therefore, silicone pacifiers can be sterilized in a microwave. The temperature should not, however, rise above 110°C/230°F. The pacifier shield is constructed of PP, a material that melts at temperatures above 110°C/230°F. Additionally, constantly subjecting pacifiers to temperatures exceeding 100°C can be hard on them and will eventually reduce their toughness and longevity.

Using electric steam sterilizing equipment to clean

Be mindful that latex will disintegrate if the temperature rises above 100°C/212°F. The use of steam sterilizer equipment for natural rubber latex pacifiers is therefore not advised. Unlike latex, silicone can withstand higher temperatures. As a result, silicone pacifiers can be sterilized using electric steam machines. However, be sure that it doesn't go above 110°C. The pacifier shield is constructed of PP, a material that melts at temperatures higher than 110°C/212°F. Similar to microwave sterilization, frequently subjecting pacifiers to temperatures exceeding 100°C can be damaging to them. Therefore, it is generally advised that you verify the manufacturer's instructions before using any steam sterilizer devices to ensure that the temperature is kept at or below 100°C/212°F.

UV sterilization devices for sterilization

We do not advise sterilizing pacifiers made of silicone or natural rubber latex using UV equipment. Natural rubber latex is a substance that ages naturally over time. This process can be altered by outside factors like UV light. Therefore, never put our latex pacifiers through a UV sterilizer because it will harm the substance. Additionally, avoid exposing natural rubber latex to the sun.

Cleaning the dishwasher for sterilization

We do not advise washing silicone or latex pacifiers in a dishwasher. Both silicone and latex materials can be harmed by cleaning solvents and become brittle. Additionally, the majority of dishwashers do not sterilize pacifiers at temperatures high enough to do so.

Soothie pacifier bad for teeth?

For all of their benefits, pacifiers do come with potential drawbacks by way of dental issues. But in most cases, when oral issues arise, it’s because a child has been using a pacifier for too long.

Crooked bites

A misaligned bite is among the most well-known dangers associated with excessive pacifier use. A crossbite, an open bite, or other malocclusions may fall under this category. Particularly when older kids are still sucking on pacifiers, these oral problems are more frequently observed. The persistent presence of a foreign item may cause a child's teeth to shift or even modify the structure of the roof of their mouth if they use pacifiers for an extended period of time.

Cavities and recession of the gums

Pacifier use has been associated, in rare instances, with pediatric caries and gingival recession, or gum loss. However, parents dipping pacifiers in sweet things is another reason for these oral diseases. Who among children wouldn't adore the delectable treat? This exposes their teeth and gums to sugar, which can promote the formation of plaque and cavities.
