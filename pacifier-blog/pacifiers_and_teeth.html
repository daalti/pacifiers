Why are pacifiers bad for teeth?

This can lead to changes in the shape of the mouth and the alignment of the teeth over time.

Another potential reason for the negative effect of pacifiers on teeth is that they may encourage a child to continue sucking on their pacifier even after their primary teeth have come in. This can lead to a condition known as "non-nutritive sucking," in which a child continues to suck on their pacifier or thumb even when they are not hungry or in need of nourishment. Non-nutritive sucking can lead to changes in the shape of the mouth and the alignment of the teeth, as well as to problems with speech development.

It is important to note that the negative effects of pacifiers on teeth and oral development can be minimized by limiting pacifier use and encouraging a child to stop using a pacifier as they get older.
