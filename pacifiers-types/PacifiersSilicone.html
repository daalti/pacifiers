Can you boil silicone pacifiers?

When a pacifier or other item is sterilized, all bacteria, even those that aren't necessarily hazardous to people, are fully eliminated. Before your infant uses a pacifier for the first time, always sterilize it to get rid of any bacteria or chemicals it might have gotten from the manufacturer.

Methods for Cleaning Pacifiers

Boiling water is one of the simplest and most well-known techniques to sanitize a pacifier.

Place the pacifier carefully in a kettle of boiling, clean water for five minutes. Make sure the water is bubbling.

Use a clean kitchen implement, such as a sizable spoon or ladle, to carefully remove. When working with hot or boiling water, exercise additional caution!

If there is any extra water in the pacifier teat, gently squeeze it flat and away from you (since the water can still be hot).

Dry with a clean towel or place on a spotless drying rack.

Difference between latex and silicone pacifiers

Natural rubber pacifiers — rubber tree from latex

Natural rubber, often known as latex, is a wholly organic substance. The juice of a rubber tree, also known as latex milk, is what is used to make natural rubber. Pacifiers made of natural rubber are soft and elastic, and they have a yellowish-brown tint.

Silicone pacifier that is free of contaminants

Silicone is a substance made in factories. The substance is frequently used in both the food and pharmaceutical industries because of its cleanliness. There are no hazardous compounds like BPA, PVC, or phthalates in silicone at all. Silicone has a smooth surface, is transparent, and is clear.
