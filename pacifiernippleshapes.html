Pacifier Shapes

Being a parent is an amazing experience. New parents, however, who are unaware of which goods are the greatest fit for their infant, may find this trip to be stressful. Every step of the journey, we're here to support you.

Babies have a natural sucking instinct from birth. The infant will make an effort to satisfy this urge by sucking on its thumb, the mother's breast, or another object. Sucking has a calming impact on the baby and serves as both a reflex for the baby to eat and a reminder of something safe and familiar in an unfamiliar environment.

Because every baby is unique, there is no right or wrong nipple size or shape. Babies differ in their preferences, methods of sucking, and mouth physiology. As a result, we provide a variety of pacifiers to accommodate these various requirements.

To discover the ideal fit for your child, it may be necessary to test out several sizes and shapes. Some babies prefer a particular nipple size, shape, or material, while others have no preferences and will accept any such size, shape, or material that is presented to them.

Pacifiers Orthodontic

The Pacifiers Orthodontic nipple is curved at the top for a natural fit to the baby's palate and has an angled tip for easy tongue placement. The curved top encourages tongue placement and sucking in which the sides of the tongue cup around the nipple, as they do during breastfeeding. Because of the angled tip, the baby does not have to suck as hard as with a round nipple in order for the pacifier to flatten inside the mouth.

The nipple has a valve that allows air to escape when the baby closes the nipple. This ventilation system pushes air from inside the nipple out through the valve, flattening the nipple and naturally resembling the baby's oral cavity. The valve is also responsible for the presence of water inside the nipple after cleaning and sterilization. If this is the case, simply flatten the nipple to squeeze out the excess water.

Round Pacifiers

Because the round shape allows the sides of the tongue to elevate and cup around the nipple, the round nipple resembles the shape and size of the mother's soft nipple and promotes a similar tongue placement and sucking technique as when breastfeeding.

Symmetrical Pacifiers

Babies who prefer the flat symmetrical nipple shape can use our pacifiers with a symmetrical flat-drop-shaped nipple. The drop-shaped nipple encourages a flat tongue placement and sucking technique, which presses the pacifier flat against the palate and eliminates the need for the baby to suck as hard as with a round nipple in order for the pacifier to flatten inside the mouth.

The nipple, like our other pacifiers, has a valve that lets out air when the baby closes the nipple. This ventilation system pushes air from inside the nipple out through the valve, flattening the nipple and naturally resembling the baby's oral cavity. The valve is also responsible for the presence of water inside the nipple after cleaning and sterilization. If this is the case, simply flatten the nipple to squeeze out the excess water.
