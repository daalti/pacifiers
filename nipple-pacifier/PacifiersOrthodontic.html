Orthodontic dummy which way up?

An orthodontic dummy, also known as a training model or manikin, is used to practice and demonstrate orthodontic techniques and procedures. It typically consists of a plastic head with teeth that are mounted on a base. The dummy is typically used in a standing position, with the base placed on a flat surface and the head upright. The teeth should be facing forward and the head should be oriented so that the front of the face is facing the user.

Which are pacifier orthodontic problems?

Pacifiers, including orthodontic pacifiers, can potentially cause problems with oral development in infants and young children if they are used improperly or for an extended period of time. Some potential problems that may be associated with pacifier use include:

1 - Thumb sucking: Prolonged thumb sucking can lead to problems with the alignment of the teeth and jaw, which can result in the need for orthodontic treatment later in life.

2 - Nipple confusion: Using a pacifier may interfere with breastfeeding, which can lead to difficulties establishing a proper latch and can result in inadequate nutrition for the infant.

3 - Delay in speech development: Pacifiers may interfere with the development of speech and language skills, as they can cause changes in the way a child produces certain sounds.

4 - Ear infections: Pacifiers may increase the risk of ear infections, as they can cause a build-up of bacteria in the mouth and upper respiratory tract.
