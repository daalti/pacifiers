Round nipple pacifier. What is?

Because the round shape allows the sides of the tongue to elevate and cup around the nipple, the round nipple resembles the shape and size of the mother's soft nipple and promotes a similar tongue placement and sucking technique as when breastfeeding.

The nipple has a valve that allows air to escape when the baby closes the nipple. This ventilation system pushes air from inside the nipple out through the valve, flattening the nipple and naturally resembling the baby's oral cavity. The valve is also responsible for the presence of water inside the nipple following cleaning and sterilization. If this is the case, simply flatten the nipple to squeeze out the excess water.

Which pacifier shape is best? Different pacifiers.

When it comes to the nipple, there is no right or wrong size or shape because every baby is unique. Babies have a variety of preferences, sucking techniques, and mouth anatomy. As a result, we offer a variety of pacifiers to meet these various requirements. To find the best fit for your child, you may need to try on different shapes and sizes. Some babies prefer a specific nipple shape, size, or material, whereas others have no preferences and will accept any shape, size, or material you offer.

Round vs Flat Pacifier. Which are the differences?

If you're worried about how a pacifier will affect your baby's oral health and bite, you should know that orthodontic or flat pacifiers may be less likely to cause bite problems in babies or toddlers. According to one study published in Progress in Orthodontics, flat pacifiers were much less likely to contribute to open bites than conventional ones. A similar conclusion was reached in a study published in the International Journal of Paediatric Dentistry , which found that traditional pacifiers were more frequently associated with open bite and overjet (sometimes known as buck teeth).

While either pacifier option will soothe your child, the type you choose may affect their bite. According to a study published in the International Journal of Paediatric Dentistry , the use of both conventional and orthodontic pacifiers increased the likelihood of malocclusion, or misaligned teeth.

Other Questions: When should you start using pacifier?

If you're breast-feeding, you might wait until your baby is 3 to 4 weeks old and you've established a nursing routine before offering a pacifier. A study of unrestricted pacifier use in healthy, full-term infants found that it had no effect on breast-feeding continuation.

We always recommend speaking with your trusted doctor if you have any concerns.

Other Question: How long do bibs pacifiers last?

Pacifiers should be replaced every 4-6 weeks for safety and hygiene reasons. Keep an eye out for any surface changes, changes in size and shape, or material ruptures, and replace the pacifier if you notice any. Pull the nipple in all directions before using it, inspect it thoroughly, replace the pacifier at the first sign of damage or weakness.

In contrast to silicone nipples, natural rubber latex is a natural material that is affected by sunlight, high temperatures, and moisture, which accelerates the aging process of the latex material. As a result, the material's properties change and it can become brittle or sticky. Whenever the material of the pacifier changes, it must be replaced.
