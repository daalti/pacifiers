Symmetrical Pacifier meaning

Babies who prefer the flat symmetrical nipple shape can use our pacifiers with a symmetrical flat-drop-shaped nipple. The drop-shaped nipple encourages a flat tongue placement and sucking technique, which presses the pacifier flat against the palate and eliminates the need for the baby to suck as hard as with a round nipple in order for the pacifier to flatten inside the mouth.

The nipple, like our other pacifiers, has a valve that lets out air when the baby closes the nipple. This ventilation system pushes air from inside the nipple out through the valve, flattening the nipple and naturally resembling the baby's oral cavity. The valve is also responsible for the presence of water inside the nipple after cleaning and sterilization. If this is the case, simply flatten the nipple to squeeze out the excess water.

Symmetrical dummy teat or pacifier teat?

A dummy teat and a pacifier teat are the same thing. They both refer to the part of a pacifier that goes in the mouth and provides a source of comfort and soothing for an infant. The term "dummy" is often used in the United Kingdom and other parts of Europe, while the term "pacifier" is more commonly used in the United States and other parts of the world. Both terms refer to a rubber, silicone, or other soft material that is shaped like a nipple and used to comfort and soothe infants.

Orthodontic vs Symmetrical Pacifiers

Orthodontic pacifiers are designed to support the proper development of an infant's teeth and jaw. They typically have a bulge or dome in the middle of the pacifier to encourage proper tongue placement and to prevent the pacifier from being pushed too far back in the mouth. Orthodontic pacifiers are often recommended by dentists and orthodontists because they can help prevent dental problems that can arise from the use of a pacifier.

Symmetrical pacifiers, on the other hand, are designed to be the same shape on both sides of the mouth. They do not have a bulge or dome in the middle and do not provide the same orthodontic benefits as orthodontic pacifiers. Some parents prefer symmetrical pacifiers because they are easier for infants to grasp and hold onto. However, they may not be as effective at preventing dental problems that can result from the use of a pacifier.
