Which Pacifier Material Is Best?

Pacifiers are typically made from one of two materials: silicone or latex. Silicone pacifiers are considered to be the safer and more durable option, as they are more resistant to tearing and can be sterilized more easily. Latex pacifiers are generally softer and more flexible, but they are not as durable and may be more prone to tearing. Additionally, some people may be allergic to latex, so silicone is generally the better choice for most people.

In latex pacifiers we have to differenciate between two: latex or rubber pacifiers and natural rubber pacifiers.

Pacifier Latex

A pacifier, also known as a dummy or a soother, is a small, often rubber or silicone nipple-shaped device that is given to a baby to suck on as a way to soothe and calm them. Latex is a type of rubber, so a pacifier made of latex would be made of a flexible, rubbery material. Some parents prefer latex pacifiers because they are softer and more pliable than other types of pacifiers, which can make them more comfortable for the baby to use. However, some people may have allergies to latex, so it is important to check with a doctor before using a latex pacifier.

Natural Rubber Pacifier

Natural rubber pacifiers are pacifiers that are made from natural rubber, a type of rubber that is derived from the sap of certain trees. Natural rubber is a popular material for pacifiers because it is soft, pliable, and safe for babies to use. Natural rubber pacifiers are often preferred by parents who want to avoid the use of synthetic materials and chemicals in their baby's products. They may also be more environmentally friendly than pacifiers made from other materials. As with any type of pacifier, it is important to regularly clean and sanitize a natural rubber pacifier to prevent the growth of bacteria and germs.

Pacifiers Silicone

Silicone is a material that is produced industrially and is noted for its purity. It is hypoallergenic, odorless, and tasteless. It is completely devoid of dangerous and endocrine-disrupting substances like BPA, PVC, and phthalates. Although the smooth, translucent nipple is not as soft as genuine rubber latex, it can tolerate high temperatures, maintain its shape, and remain elastic.

There are two distinct silicone types. 1. silicon (food-grade) for products that come into contact with food. Silicone for medical implants and pharmaceutical items (medical-grade). The utilization of the two grades is what distinguishes them most:

- For products that come into contact with food, such as silicone pacifier nipples in this example, where particularly high tensile values are crucial, food-grade silicone is employed.

- The biocompatibility of medical-grade silicone, which is utilized for pharmaceutical items and implant devices but not for pacifier nipples, is examined.Medical-grade silicone does not automatically translate to higher product quality or superior mechanical performance. Contrarily, medical-grade silicone may even cause the strength values, which are crucial for pacifier nipples, to decrease.

Both grades are produced using strict quality control techniques to adhere to the law and guarantee no harm is done to people or the environment. Food-grade silicone is used to create our silicone nipples. We still advise replacing your pacifiers every 4-6 weeks for sanitary reasons.
