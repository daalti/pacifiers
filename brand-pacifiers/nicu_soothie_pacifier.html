Difference between pacifier and soothie

A pacifier and a soothie are both products that are designed to soothe and comfort infants and young children. However, there are some differences between the two.

One difference is the material that they are made from. Pacifiers are often made from silicone, latex, or rubber, while soothies are typically made from medical-grade silicone.

Another difference is the shape of the nipple. Pacifiers may have a variety of different shaped nipples, such as orthodontic, butterfly, or round, while soothies are designed to mimic the shape and movement of a mother's breast.

A third difference is the intended use. Pacifiers are generally used to soothe and comfort infants and young children during times of stress or when they are trying to fall asleep. Soothies, on the other hand, are specifically designed for use in the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) and are intended to provide comfort and security for newborns.

Overall, pacifiers and soothies are both useful tools for soothing and comforting infants and young children, but they differ in the materials they are made from, the shape of the nipple, and their intended use.
